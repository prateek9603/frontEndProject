History Of The Pink City - Jaipur

Birth Of Jaipur

The history of Jaipur began when the Jaipur Kingdom was formed after succeeding the vibrant and prosperous kingdom of Amber. The colourful city was founded by Maharaja Jai Singh II, also known as Sawai Jai Singh in 1727. No doubt that Jaipur is a well-designed city as its founder was a famed mathematician and astronomer. Vidyadhar Bhattacharya was a Brahmin Scholar from Bengal who aided Sawai Jai Singh in laying out the city's architecture. Major places, roads and squares took 4 years for their completion and each was constructed keeping in mind the techniques of Vastu Shastra.

Power Struggles

Jaipur might have struggled to be in power but it continued to be a princely state till 1948. The history of Jaipur speaks about the internal power struggles in the Pink City. Though, it had lost against Marathas in the Battle of Patan fought in 1790, it had enough wealth to carry its patronage. After Sawai Jai Singh, came Ishwari Singh, followed by different rulers belonging to Kachwaha dynasty. Jaipur became a part of Indian Union on 7 April 1949. Presently, Sawai Padmanabh Singh and his Royal Family live in The City Palace of Jaipur.

Why Pink City ?

In 1876, the Prince of Wales and Queen Victoria were to pay a visit to India. Pink signifies hospitality, so to bring to life the essence of welcoming words, "Padharo Mahrey Desh", Maharajah Ram Singh got the city drenched in this beautiful colour. The colour, Terracotta Pink adds seven stars to the pride of this majestic city. Even today if you visit Jaipur, you will find the historical gates and squares that have now turned into bazaars possessing the colour of hospitality and has woven itself into the history of Jaipur

Why you should Visit Jaipur?

There are several compelling reasons why everyone should consider visiting Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan.

Rich Cultural Heritage: Jaipur is steeped in history and boasts a rich cultural heritage. It is home to numerous forts, palaces, temples, and museums that offer insights into the royal past of Rajasthan and India. Visitors can experience the grandeur and opulence of the city's architectural wonders.

Magnificent Architecture: The architecture of Jaipur is awe-inspiring. From the intricate designs of Hawa Mahal to the grandeur of Amber Fort, the city showcases a blend of Rajasthani, Mughal, and other architectural styles, making it a paradise for architecture enthusiasts.

Vibrant Markets: Jaipur's markets are a shopper's delight. From colorful textiles, traditional handicrafts, and jewelry to spices and unique souvenirs, the markets offer a wide range of products that reflect the city's rich culture and craftsmanship.

Warm Hospitality: Rajasthan is known for its warm hospitality, and Jaipur is no exception. Visitors can experience the famous Rajasthani hospitality through the welcoming locals, cultural shows, and heritage hotels that provide a taste of royal living.

Culinary Delights: Jaipur's cuisine is a delightful blend of spicy and flavorful dishes. From traditional Rajasthani thalis to delectable street food, foodies will be in for a treat as they explore the city's culinary offerings.

Fascinating Festivals: Jaipur hosts numerous festivals throughout the year, each offering a unique experience. The city comes alive during festivals like Diwali, Holi, Teej, and the Jaipur Literature Festival, providing visitors with an opportunity to immerse themselves in the local culture and traditions

Palatial Hotels: Jaipur is home to some of the most luxurious and historically significant hotels in India. Staying in these palatial hotels, such as the Taj Rambagh Palace or the Oberoi Rajvilas, offers a chance to experience the royal lifestyle of the past.

Colorful and Vibrant City: Jaipur's moniker, the "Pink City," comes from its distinct pink-colored buildings. The vibrant cityscape, bustling streets, and lively atmosphere add to the overall charm of the city.

Art and Crafts: Jaipur is a hub for various traditional art forms and crafts. Visitors can witness artisans at work, creating intricate block prints, pottery, jewelry, and more, showcasing the city's artistic legacy.

Gateway to Rajasthan: As the capital of Rajasthan, Jaipur serves as an excellent starting point for exploring other fascinating destinations in the state, such as Jodhpur, Udaipur, Pushkar, and the Thar Desert

Whether you are interested in history, architecture, culture, or simply want to experience the unique flavors of Rajasthan, Jaipur has something to offer to every traveler. It is a city that leaves a lasting impression on visitors and provides a memorable and enriching travel experience
